TRANSPORTATION

TRANSPORTATION

Getting around Helsinki and the Capital region is quite easy. Downtown you can do everything by foot as the center is not that big. However, if you want to venture outside the city center or want to save time public transport is the fastest, cheapest and easiest way to get around. With a HSL day ticket you have 24 hours unlimited access to the trams, busses and trains in the Helsinki. Other ways of transportation can be by taxi or car, with cars we can provide you with a rental car for yourself or we can provide a chauffeur make it a bit easier for you.

MUSEUMS

MUSEUMS

Helsinki has many museums, but we choose the most impressive one’s for you to make the choice a bit easier. The first one is Kiasma, located downtown Helsinki is one of the leading contemporary art museums in all of the Nordics. Secondly Ateneum is considered to be the home of Finnish art, so to truly get a taste of the Finnish art scene this is a wonderful place to see. To find out more about these museums and other beautiful ones in Helsinki click on read more.

SUOMENLINNA

SUOMENLINNA

A Unesco World Heritage Site, this is one of Finland’s most popular attractions. This fortress is a great place to visit all year round but especially in spring and summer. With its restaurants, museums and events Suomenlinna offers a great experience for everyone. Explore “The Fortress of Finland” and travel through Finnish history.

KORKEASAARI ZOO

KORKEASAARI ZOO

Founded in 1889, Korkeasaari Zoo is one of the oldest ones in the world. Located on its own small island next to Helsinki, it is a beautiful place to visit. Every season brings you a different zoo as they are open all year round, from a beautiful winter wonderland to a fresh new zoo with new life in spring.

COSY CAFÉS

COSY CAFÉS

Did you know that Finns are the largest coffee consumers in the world? Thanks to this there are lots of cozy and nice cafés and coffee shops in around the city. But the one standing out most is definitely Café Regatta, dating back to the 19th century it is one the most charming cafés in Helsinki. This small red wooden cabin located next to the seaside it the perfect place for a warm cup of coffee and some tasty traditional blueberry pie.

TIME TO RELAX

TIME TO RELAX

Finns love sauna and jumping into a cold lake, river or the sea afterwards. But to make it a bit more comfortable we advise going to one of Helsinki’s swimming pools. These are greatest places to relax by taking a dive in one pools or by enjoying the warmth of a hot sauna. Some of best pools are Allas Sea Pool a garden like oasis in the harbor of Helsinki and Yrjönkatu Swimming Hall the oldest public pool of Helsinki that makes you feel like you stepped into a Wes Anderson movie. Besides pools and saunas these places also have cafés, and more to make sure you will leave them completely relax and fresh.

HELSINKI'S CUISINE

HELSINKI'S CUISINE

From traditional Finnish or even Lappish cuisine to delicious modern fusion restaurants, Helsinki has it all. If you want to experience a typical Finnish dinner Ravintola (restaurant) Aino, this is one of the best traditional serving restaurants in Helsinki. For the wild taste of Lapland Saaga is the place to visit, their menu is inspired by Lappish nature and a this is great place to try reindeer. Modern and fusion are also very popular in Helsinki, with most of them inspired or influenced by the European and Asian kitchen. Some very good ones are Farang, Passio, Copas y Tapas and Nokka.

NATURE

NATURE

Finland has a lot of beautiful lakes, forests, fjells and national parks. Nature is so vast in the country that even in the capital area you can find the most amazing places to go hiking, cycling or do any other outdoor activity. Near Helsinki you find Nuuksio a beautiful national park. Every season brings you a different experience. Enjoy a day in the nature and treat yourself outdoors after an active day with some sausages cooked over a fire in a typical Finnish “kota” (hut).
